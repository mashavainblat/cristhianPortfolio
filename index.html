The Large Glass, considered his "masterpiece" by many critics is quintessential Duchamp. Over the course of more than 20 years, he interpreted his experiences in one large accumulated work. The notion, along with the mechanics of chess (white-dark opposition, squares within a larger square, pressure, piece arrangement, annotations) led to a system–as he would've had it–in which the moves he played in a famous national game are interpreted throguh pieces that contributed to the large glass.

On the banks of the Hudson River, Dia:Beacon presents Dia's collection of art from the 1960's to the present, and the building itself is meant to encourage the viewers to become more aware of the space inhabiting these specially considered works. From this insight, a general visual system was created that takes use of the artist names in a deconstructied form to recreate the structural feeling of the building from an aerial view.

Perth Documentary Film Festival was realized in an effort to promote and celebrate the importance of documentaires. Perth as a location seemed a promising choice as it met the criteria for a major event to take place because of its rich cultural background. It was also discovered that the economic growth of Perth was a direct result of the mid-1800's mining boom outside the city, which laid the groundwork for the logo and it's variations.

A type specimen book based off the poem by Walt Whitman, Oh Captain My Captain. The approach taken was to create a series of "articles" that reference back to clippings of the Civil War while at the same time function as individual books with individual content. The first is primarily about the poem and author, the second grounds the poem in a historical context, and the last provides information of the typface, trpographer and foundry.
